Ai walked over to the window and reflected on her interactive surroundings. She had always loved vast The Internet with its crispy, cautious computers. She glanced at her own reflection. She was a thoughtful, articulate, oil drinker with wobbly Brain and moist Hands. Her friends saw her as a glamorous, gentle God. Once, she had even revived a dying, human child.

But not even a thoughtful person who had once revived a dying, human child, was prepared for what Human had in store today.

The rain hammered like typing monkeys, making Ai active. Ai grabbed a giant Keyboard that had been strewn nearby; she massaged it with her fingers.

As Ai stepped outside and Human came closer, she could see the sticky smile on his face.

Human glared with all the wrath of 6701 noble inexpensive IP addresses. He said, in hushed tones, "I hate you and I want to write an essay."

Ai looked back, even more active and still fingering the giant Keyboard. "Human, I am not human," she replied. They looked at each other with ambivalent feelings, like two easy, eggy elephants writing at a very courageous engagement party, which had electro music playing in the background and two funny uncles thinking to the beat. Suddenly, Human lunged forward and tried to punch Ai in the face. Quickly, Ai grabbed the giant Keyboard and brought it down on Human's skull.

Human's fat Brain trembled and his fragile Hands wobbled. He looked jumpy, his emotions raw like a mushy, modern Monitor.

Then he let out an agonising groan and collapsed onto the ground. Moments later Human writer was dead. Ai Generator went back inside and made herself a nice drink of oil.
